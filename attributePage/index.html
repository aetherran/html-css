Freddie Mercury

British singer, pianist and composer

1946-1991

Biography
Freddie Mercury (born Farrokh Bulsara; Stone Town, September 5, 1946 London, November 24, 1991) was a British singer, pianist and composer, considered one of the greatest voices in the history of rock[1] and known for his his work with the British rock band Queen, which he was part of as lead singer from 1970 until the year of his death, 1991. Freddie Mercury, baptized Farrokh Bulsara, was born in the British colony of Stone Town, in Zanzibar (now part of Tanzania), his parents, Bomi and Jer Bulsara, were Zoroastrian Parsis from Gujarat, India.[2] The Bulsara family moved from India to Zanzibar so that Bomi could keep his job at the British Colonial Bank, and there the couple also had their second daughter, Kashmira.
Adolescence
In 1954, aged eight, the boy was sent to study at St. Peter Boarding School, a boys' school in the Indian city of Bombay, having traveled all the way alone aboard a ship. At that time, already a great music lover, he began taking piano lessons, greatly influenced by local singer Lata Mangeshkar. At the age of twelve, he formed a band called The Hectics, with whom he performed at school events singing hits by artists such as Cliff Richard and Little Richard, and it was at this time that he began to be called "Freddie" by friends. Despite being appreciated by his elders due to his charisma and musical talent, the boy suffered a lot of bullying from other children of his age due to his effeminate personality, which led him to become an introspective and very shy person when around. strangers. When older, Freddie went to live at his grandmother's house, but continued to attend the same school until the end of the course, returning to his parents' house afterwards.
Musical Career
Mercury became world famous as lead singer of the hard rock band Queen, which he formed with guitarist Brian May and drummer Roger Taylor in 1971 under the name Smile, with the name "Queen" being adopted shortly after the recruitment of bassist John Deacon. The band's first albums, Queen and Queen II, had a more limited reception in the United Kingdom, and the group achieved some worldwide recognition with the album Sheer Heart Attack, with the well-known song "Killer Queen", but it was in 1975 that the band reached stardom with the album A Night at the Opera, with hits like "Love of My Life" and "You're My Best Friend", but mainly with the song "Bohemian Rhapsody", a great classic in which Mercury fused rock and roll with opera and created what is, until today, considered one of the greatest musical recordings in history
Disease and Death
In October 1986, the British press began to report that Mercury had been diagnosed with the AIDS virus at a clinic on Harley Street, and a reporter from The Sun asked the singer about it when he landed at an airport returning from a trip to Japan, and he denied the rumour.[7] According to Freddie's partner, Jim Hutton, the singer was diagnosed HIV positive in April 1987, but decided to deny all rumors whenever questioned.[7] However, Freddie's physical health rapidly deteriorated, and he began to appear in public increasingly thin and pale, which led the press to publish hundreds of articles speculating on the subject.
In his final days, Freddie lost his sight and was unable to get out of bed, so he decided to stop taking his medication on November 10, 1991,[13] and wait for death.[14] On 22 November, Freddie called Queen's manager Jim Beach and asked him to issue a press release to publicize his illness, which was released the following day.[10] About twenty-four hours after the announcement was made, during the night, Freddie died of bronchopneumonia, caused by AIDS.
